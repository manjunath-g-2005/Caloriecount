Metabolic Science 101

This tool uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the gold standard in clinical dietetics. However, biology is not a calculator. Your actual results will depend on Hormonal Health, NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity), and Thermic Effect of Food.

Weight Loss (Cutting)

Ideally, aim for a deficit of 300-500 kcal. More aggressive cuts (>700 kcal) risk muscle loss and metabolic adaptation.

Muscle Gain (Bulking)

Muscle grows slowly. A surplus of 200-300 kcal is ideal ("Lean Bulk"). Dirty bulking (>500 surplus) often results in unnecessary fat gain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I gain muscle and lose fat?

Yes, this is called "Body Recomposition." It works best for beginners or those returning to training. Set the slider to "Maintenance" or a very slight deficit (-100).

How much protein do I need?

For muscle retention or growth, aim for 1.6g to 2.2g of protein per kg of body weight.
